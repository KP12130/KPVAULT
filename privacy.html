KPVAULT Privacy Policy — Last updated: April 2026

Zero Data Collection Policy
The short version: KPVAULT never collects, transmits, or sells your personal data. Everything is encrypted locally on your device using keys that only you control. We have no access to your passwords, 2FA secrets, or recovery phrase — ever.

1. Who We Are

KPVAULT is an independent mobile application for secure, local-first password and two-factor authentication management. The app is developed and maintained as a personal privacy tool.

2. What Data We Collect

Personal Information

None. We do not collect your name, email, phone number, or any identifying information.

Passwords & 2FA Secrets

All credentials are stored exclusively on your device using React Native's AsyncStorage and encrypted using AES-256. We never read, transmit, or see your stored credentials.

Recovery Phrase (Seed)

Your 12-word recovery phrase is generated locally using cryptographically secure randomness. It is never transmitted to any server. It is purged from memory immediately after setup.

Analytics & Crash Reporting

We do not use any analytics SDK, crash reporting service, or advertising framework. No telemetry of any kind is collected.

3. Cloud Sync (Optional)

KPVAULT offers an optional zero-knowledge cloud sync feature. If you choose to use it:

  • Your vault data is AES-256 encrypted locally using a key derived from your recovery phrase before being uploaded.
  • The encryption key is derived using PBKDF2 and never leaves your device.
  • Your Firebase document is identified only by a SHA-256 hash of your seed phrase — no username, email, or account is required.
  • We (and Firebase/Google) cannot decrypt your data. Only someone with your recovery phrase can.
  • If you do not use cloud sync, no data ever leaves your device.
Firebase (Google) is the cloud storage provider. Their infrastructure-level privacy policy applies to the encrypted blob stored. They cannot access its contents.

4. Biometric Data

KPVAULT may use your device's biometric hardware (fingerprint, Face ID) for authentication. This is handled entirely by the device operating system through the expo-local-authentication library. Biometric data never leaves your device and is never accessible to KPVAULT.

5. Data Export

The app provides an optional "Export Vault as JSON" feature. This creates a local file on your device containing your credentials in plain text. You are solely responsible for the security of exported files. We recommend encrypting any exported backup before storing it externally.

⚠️ Exported files are not encrypted. Store and share them with extreme caution.

6. Third-Party Services

KPVAULT uses the following third-party services or libraries:

  • Firebase Firestore (optional cloud sync) — Google's privacy policy applies to encrypted blobs stored on their infrastructure.
  • expo-local-authentication — Biometric auth via the device OS. No data leaves the device.
  • crypto-js — Local AES-256 encryption library. No network calls.
  • otplib / totp-generator — Local TOTP code generation. No network calls.
